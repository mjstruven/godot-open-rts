HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT
This is your complete instruction set for building The Art of War MVP in Godot 4 using the godot-open-rts template as the foundation. Read it fully before writing any code. When in doubt about a game design decision, refer to the GDD (Game Design Document v0.3). When in doubt about implementation, follow the patterns already established in the existing codebase.
Your working directory is the godot-open-rts project. Do not create a new project. Extend and modify what exists.
Your guiding principle throughout: Build the simplest version of each system that proves the concept works. This is an MVP. Playable and correct beats polished and complex.

WHAT YOU ARE BUILDING
A 1v1 skirmish RTS where two players build economies, field armies, and capture the enemy capital. The game ends when one player captures the opponent's active capital building and the opponent fails to designate a new capital within 2 minutes.
MVP scope is intentionally narrow. If something is not listed in the BUILD sections below, do not build it. Log it as a future feature comment instead.

SECTION 1 — UNIT ROSTER
Implement the following unit types by extending the existing Unit base class. Keep stats in external resource files (.tres or .gd constants file) so they can be tuned without touching code.
Personnel Units
Infantry
* HP: 6 | Size: 1 | Damage: 2 | Attack Range: 1 tile | Attack Rate: 1 per 2 seconds | Move Speed: 1.25 tiles/sec
* Upkeep: 4 food + 1 gold per minute
* Special ability — Bolster: Unit stops moving and raises spears. Gains +5 temporary HP and deals 2 reflect damage to melee attackers. Lasts until unit moves. 5 second cooldown after deactivation.
* Role: General purpose. Required to crew siege equipment and garrison/capture buildings.
Archer
* HP: 4 | Size: 1 | Damage: 6 | Attack Range: 10 tiles | Attack Rate: 1 per 6 seconds | Move Speed: 1.0 tiles/sec
* AOE attack: Each volley targets a 3x3 tile zone. Each arrow has a 40% chance to hit each unit in the zone. Multiple archers firing at the same zone add their arrows independently (stacking hit probability per unit in zone). Friendly fire enabled.
* Upkeep: 6 food + 2 gold per minute
* Special ability — Fire at Will: Attack cooldown reduced 50% for 24 seconds. Unit cannot move while active.
Cavalry
* HP: 16 | Size: 2 | Damage: 4 | Attack Range: 1 tile | Attack Rate: 1 per 2 seconds | Move Speed: 2.0 tiles/sec
* Upkeep: 8 food + 5 gold per minute
* Special ability — Charge: Dash in a straight line toward target. +1 tile/sec speed, +6 damage, +1 tile trample AOE for 5 seconds. 20 second cooldown.
Engineer
* HP: 6 | Size: 1 | Damage: 1 | Attack Range: 1 tile | Attack Rate: 1 per 2 seconds | Move Speed: 1.0 tiles/sec
* No upkeep cost
* Special ability — Repair: When assigned to a damaged building, repairs it at 4 HP/second. Costs 1 wood per 10 HP repaired. Automatically cancels a building's scuttle order if building HP is above 25%.
* Can construct all buildings (see Section 2).
Mercenary
* HP: 6 | Size: 1 | Damage: 3 | Attack Range: 1 tile | Attack Rate: 1 per 2 seconds | Move Speed: 1.25 tiles/sec
* Upkeep: 10 food + 8 gold per minute (expensive — meant to be dismissed after use)
* No special ability
* Trains in 3 seconds (vs 6 for infantry). Build cost: 50 gold.
Flag Commander
* HP: 32 | Size: 2 | Damage: 4 | Attack Range: 1 tile | Attack Rate: 1 per 2 seconds
* Move Speed: Matches speed of any group it is assigned to command. Default: 2.25 tiles/sec
* Maximum 4 per player
* Special ability — Rally: All allied units within 8 tiles gain +20% movement speed and +20% attack rate for 15 seconds. 45 second cooldown.
Supply Wagon
* HP: 32 | Size: 6 | Damage: 0 | Move Speed: 1.0 tiles/sec
* Passive aura: All allied units within 8 tiles gain +5% to all combat stats
* Cannot attack
Siege Units
MVP NOTE: Implement Battering Ram and Trebuchet first. Add others (Ballista, Mangonel, Siege Ladder, Siege Tower) in subsequent passes. Mark unimplemented siege as TODO comments.
Battering Ram
* HP: 64 | Size: 4 | Damage: 0 vs units, 100 vs gates and towers | Attack Rate: 1 per 6 seconds | Move Speed: 1.0 tiles/sec
* Build cost: 100 wood + 50 gold | Crew: 5 infantry minimum to operate
* Can transport up to 20 size-1 units inside for protection
* Immune to archer damage
* When destroyed, all loaded troops are destroyed with it
Trebuchet
* HP: 32 | Size: 6 | Damage: 32 | Attack Range: 10–20 tiles (25 tiles inaccurate range) | Attack Rate: 1 per 10 seconds | AOE: 3x3 tiles
* Build cost: 300 wood + 200 gold | Crew: 5 infantry minimum to operate
* Can only target buildings or attack-ground. Cannot target units directly.
* Pack/unpack time: 30 seconds (must unpack to fire, must pack to move)
* Accuracy degrades beyond 20 tiles — projectile scatters within a 3-tile radius of target
* Units on struck wall sections also take damage
* Move Speed: 1.0 tiles/sec (when packed)
* Special ability — Flying Fire: Next 3 attacks leave burning oil at impact site dealing 6 damage/second for 20 seconds in 2x2 tile AOE

SECTION 2 — BUILDINGS
All buildings are placed by Engineers. Buildings snap to the tile grid. Implement building placement using the existing structure system in the template as a base.
Core Data — All Buildings Share These Behaviors:
Efficiency degradation by HP:
* 76–100% HP → 100% efficiency (full output)
* 51–75% HP → 85% efficiency
* 26–50% HP → 65% efficiency
* 1–25% HP → 40% efficiency (fire begins at this threshold)
Fire:
* Buildings at or below 25% HP automatically begin burning
* Fire deals 2 HP/second passive damage
* Fire stops when building is repaired above 25% HP threshold
* Building at 0 HP → becomes a Ruin (impassable, no function, clearable by engineer in 60 seconds)
Scuttle (self-destruct):
* Any player can order their own building to scuttle via right-click context menu
* Scuttling immediately begins fire damage at 2 HP/sec regardless of current HP
* Visually identical to normal fire — enemy cannot distinguish scuttle from combat damage
* Canceled by engineer repair if building is above 25% HP
* Below 25% HP, fire continues even if scuttle is canceled — must repair past threshold to extinguish
Capture:
* When a capital is captured, all buildings within 12-tile radius immediately transfer to attacker
* Transferred buildings: production queues cancel, training resets (even 90% complete units are lost)
* Buildings mid-construction within radius flip to attacker if >10% complete; abandoned if ≤10%
* Units in the field are NOT affected by building capture
Building List
Capital / Government Center
* HP: 800 | Build cost: 600 wood + 400 stone | Footprint: 4x4 tiles
* Cannot be destroyed — can only be captured (immune to all damage)
* Captured by garrisoning 30 enemy infantry inside for 3 uninterrupted minutes
* Garrison progress bar visible to both players on the building
* Progress resets if infantry count drops below 30 at any moment
* On capture: triggers 2-minute relocation window for losing player (see Section 4 — Win Conditions)
* Capital bonus: All buildings within 12-tile radius gain +20% resource output and +20% production speed
* Show capital influence radius as a subtle ground overlay
Town Center
* HP: 400 | Build cost: 300 wood + 200 stone | Footprint: 3x3 tiles
* Maximum 3 town centers per player at all times (including active capital)
* Can be designated as active capital for 300 gold + 200 stone (10 second conversion, instant during relocation window)
* Trains: Infantry, Archers, Cavalry, Mercenaries
* Training rates: Infantry 1/6 sec, Archer 1/12 sec, Cavalry 1/12 sec, Mercenary 1/3 sec
Academy
* HP: 80 | Build cost: 150 wood + 50 stone | Footprint: 2x2 tiles
* Trains: Infantry, Archers, Cavalry
* Same training rates as Town Center
Siege Workshop
* HP: 80 | Build cost: 200 wood + 100 stone | Footprint: 3x3 tiles
* Produces all siege equipment
Grain Mill
* HP: 40 | Build cost: 100 wood | Footprint: 2x2 tiles
* Must be placed on a Fertile Land terrain tile
* Produces: 60 food/minute (modified by efficiency and capital bonus)
* Supply wagon spawns automatically and travels to nearest depot or capital
Lumber Mill
* HP: 40 | Build cost: 100 wood | Footprint: 2x2 tiles
* Must be placed on a Forest terrain tile
* Produces: 60 wood/minute (modified by efficiency)
Stone Mill
* HP: 40 | Build cost: 100 wood | Footprint: 2x2 tiles
* Must be placed on a Rocky/Mountain terrain tile
* Produces: 60 stone/minute (modified by efficiency)
House
* HP: 20 | Build cost: 50 wood | Footprint: 1x1 tiles
* Increases civilian housing capacity (enables civilian population growth)
Manor
* HP: 60 | Build cost: 150 wood + 50 stone | Footprint: 2x2 tiles
* Functions as: Academy + Watch Tower + House combined
* Must be placed outside capital radius AND within a Grain Mill's radius
Watch Tower
* HP: 80 | Build cost: 80 wood + 40 stone | Footprint: 1x1 tiles
* Auto-fires arrows equivalent to 5 archers (same AOE volley system)
* Buildable anywhere on map
Wall (per tile segment)
* HP: 40 | Build cost: 50 wood + 25 stone per tile | Footprint: 1 tile wide
* Only damageable by trebuchet or engineer sappers (future feature — flag as TODO)
* 4 size-1 units can garrison each wall tile
* Garrisoned units gain 50% dodge chance vs ranged attacks
* Walls can be scuttled
Gate
* HP: 100 | Build cost: 80 wood + 40 stone | Footprint: 2x1 tiles
* Only damageable by trebuchet or battering ram
* Side towers auto-fire (5 archer equivalent)
* Opens/closes by owning player command
Wall Tower
* HP: 120 | Build cost: 100 wood + 50 stone | Footprint: 1x1 tiles
* Maximum 1 per 5 wall tiles
* Auto-fires arrows (5 archer equivalent)
Supply Depot
* HP: 60 | Build cost: 80 wood + 40 stone | Footprint: 2x2 tiles
* Relay point for supply chains from distant resource nodes
* Stores up to 500 of each resource as a buffer
MVP NOTE: Keep (Castle), Trade Workshop are future scope. Add as TODO stubs.

SECTION 3 — ECONOMY SYSTEM
Resources
Four resource types: Food, Wood, Stone, Gold.
Display in HUD as: current stockpile | income per minute | expenditure per minute | net per minute. Resources update once per minute (synced to supply chain arrival pulse). The stockpile number ticks up visually once per minute, not continuously.
Resource Collection
* Resource mills produce a trickle once per minute when connected to a supply chain
* Base rate: 60 of the relevant resource per minute per mill
* Modified by: building efficiency (HP-based), capital bonus (+20% if in radius)
* Supply chain: an automated wagon entity travels from the mill to the nearest Supply Depot or Capital. On arrival it delivers the cargo. It then returns. Round trip = 1 minute at base speed.
* If a supply wagon's route is blocked or the wagon is destroyed for more than 2 continuous minutes, that mill's trickle suspends until route is restored.
* Supply wagon stats: HP 8, move speed 2.0 tiles/sec, respawns at source mill after 60 seconds if destroyed
Gold
* Gold is not collected by buildings
* Gold trickles in passively based on civilian population
* Placeholder formula: 1 gold per minute per 100 civilians
* Civilian population grows automatically when housing capacity exists and food stockpile is positive
* Do not implement full civilian simulation for MVP — use a simplified counter that grows by 10 civilians/minute when food net income is positive, shrinks by 10/minute when food is in deficit
Army Upkeep
Deducted from stockpile once per minute:
* Infantry: 4 food + 1 gold
* Archer: 6 food + 2 gold
* Cavalry: 8 food + 5 gold
* Mercenary: 10 food + 8 gold
* Siege (battering ram, trebuchet): 5 gold each
If a resource stockpile hits zero and upkeep cannot be paid, apply a -10% combat penalty to all units (resource deficit debuff). Show a clear HUD warning.
Raiding Resource Nodes
When an enemy destroys, raids, or captures a resource building:
* Destroy: Remove 5% of the target player's total stockpile of that resource. Fast action, no troops required beyond combat.
* Raid: Requires 10 infantry assigned. Takes 60 seconds. Removes 5% of enemy stockpile and transfers 2.5% to raider's stockpile via next supply chain delivery.
* Capture: Requires 25 infantry assigned. Takes 120 seconds. Removes 5% of enemy stockpile and transfers it all to attacker. Building changes ownership and connects to attacker's supply network.

SECTION 4 — WIN CONDITIONS
Primary Win Condition: Capital Capture
1. Attacker assigns 30+ infantry to garrison inside enemy Capital building
2. Garrison progress fills over 3 minutes (progress bar visible on building to both players)
3. Progress resets instantly if infantry count drops below 30 for any reason
4. On successful capture: capital and all buildings in 12-tile radius transfer to attacker immediately
Relocation Window
5. Losing player gets a 2-minute countdown timer displayed prominently on screen (large, center-top)
6. Player must designate a new capital before timer expires by:
   * Converting an existing Town Center (300 gold + 200 stone, 10 seconds during relocation window)
   * OR completing construction of a new Town Center and designating it
7. If new capital is designated: game continues, timer disappears
8. If timer expires with no new capital: player is defeated — show defeat screen
Loss Conditions
* Relocation timer expires
* Player surrenders (menu option available at any time)
Implementation Notes
* Track garrison progress as a float 0.0 to 1.0 per capital building instance
* Each tick with 30+ infantry inside: progress += delta / 180.0 (180 seconds total)
* Progress resets to 0.0 when infantry count drops below 30
* Town center count is enforced as a hard cap of 3 (including the active capital)

When calculating damage, effective damage = base damage × (1.0 + sum of all positive modifiers - sum of all negative modifiers). SECTION 10 — AI OPPONENT
Extend the existing AI player system in the template. The MVP AI does not need to be good — it needs to be functional enough to playtest the game systems against something.
MVP AI Behavior (Priority Order)
1. Build economy first: Construct 1 Grain Mill, 1 Lumber Mill, 1 Stone Mill as first actions
2. Train infantry: Begin training infantry at Town Center once mills are built
3. Attack when ready: When army reaches 20+ infantry, march toward enemy capital
4. Don't manage supply wagons: AI treats resource income as automatic (simplification for MVP)
5. React to attacks: If own buildings are attacked and army is idle, send army to defend
The AI should not manage morale, upkeep optimization, or flanking for MVP. It is a placeholder to make the game testable.

SECTION 12 — CODING STANDARDS
Follow the patterns already established in the godot-open-rts codebase. Specifically:
* GDScript only. No C#.
* Scene-based architecture. Every unit type, building type, and UI panel is its own .tscn scene with an attached .gd script.
* Signals for communication. Systems communicate via Godot signals, not direct references where avoidable. Follow the existing signal patterns in Match.gd.
* Resource files for data. Unit stats, building stats, and modifier values live in .tres Resource files or a central GameData.gd constants file — not hardcoded in scripts.
* No magic numbers. All tunable values (damage, range, upkeep costs, timer durations) must be named constants or exported variables.
* Comments on non-obvious logic. The modifier stack calculation, garrison progress, and supply chain logic particularly need clear inline comments.
* One responsibility per script. A unit's movement script does not also handle its combat. Follow the existing separation of concerns.
File Naming Convention
Follow existing conventions in the project:
* Scenes: PascalCase.tscn
* Scripts: snake_case.gd
* Resource files: unit_infantry.tres, building_grain_mill.tres etc.

SECTION 13 — BUILD ORDER RECOMMENDATION
Build in this sequence. Each milestone should produce something runnable:
Phase 1 — Get units on the map Add Infantry, Archer, Cavalry as unit types. Get them selecting, moving, and attacking each other using the existing template framework. Confirm fog of war works per unit.
Phase 2 — Economy Add resource mills, supply wagons, the resource HUD bar, upkeep deduction. Confirm resources tick correctly and deficit applies combat penalty.
Phase 3 — Buildings and placement Add all buildings. Engineer construction. Scuttle and fire mechanic. Building capture on capital fall.
Phase 4 — Capital system and win condition Garrison mechanic, capture transfer, relocation timer, defeat condition. This is the game loop.
Phase 5 — Combat modifiers and morale Modifier stack system, terrain effects, morale score and HUD display.
Phase 6 — AI opponent Basic AI that builds economy and attacks. Enough to playtest against.
Phase 7 — Weather and remaining units Weather events, remaining personnel units (Engineer, Mercenary, Flag Commander, Supply Wagon), Trebuchet and Battering Ram.
Phase 8 — Polish pass HUD completeness, alerts, sound stubs, basic visual feedback on fire/scuttle/garrison progress.
